Eamonn O'Brien-Strain is a senior technologist and technical leader with deep and broad experience as a system architect, project manager, agile coach, and computer science researcher. He has worked in many application areas including embedded systems, hardware-software design automation, video authoring tools, print design tools, scalable web applications, and web page analysis.

At HP Labs, O'Brien-Strain is a principal engineer acting as the software architect for Automated Publishing where he lays the foundation for integrating research technologies into scalable cloud services and transferring them to the product divisions. He has championed agile processes and engineering practices on several projects and personally assembled and guided agile teams to successful, high quality releases of Autophotobook SaaS and HP Clipper. He has worked on pervasive computing, synthesis of video from consumer media, designing platforms to advance research in multimedia algorithms, and the use of on-line casual games to help organize personal photo collections.

Before his current role as a researcher and system architect at HP Labs, he was an early part of the development team at two start-ups. He also built chip and system design automation tools at various companies including GEC-Marconi Research in England, AT&T Bell Labs in New Jersey, and Cadence Design Systems in California.

O'Brien-Strain chased the Silicon Valley dream in several start-ups, the latest of which was an Internet company providing a light-weight project management and collaboration platform for freelancers and small business, which HP acquired in 2001.

His open-source projects include Glawn, a very simple fully-static CMS and web-site builder and Nrby Photos a Web OS app has had more than 10,000 downloads (despite still being in beta and not in the app store) and a four-star rating.

He has twelve granted patents with more in the pipeline.

Granted U.S. Patents

7,797,359 Recursive data naming
This patent addresses the problem of how to build a data-store overlay on a radically-distributed ensemble of devices. It combines aspects of DNS, distributed-file systems, REST, and key-value noSQL stores. It is designed to have no single points of failure, to be resilient to network partitions, and to have no central authority.

8,132,096 Image composition evaluation
Represent rules of photo composition (e.g. the rule of thirds) as a grayscale map. Represent how salient different parts of a photograph are by another grayscale map. Compare the two maps to find out how compositionally good the photo is, or shift the maps relative to each other so that the salient areas line up with the best areas compositionally, and thus get a nice cropping of a photograph.
8,301,669 Concurrent presentation of video segments enabling rapid video file comprehension
This is an array of short looping video thumbnails that move so that end of loop flows seamlessly into the beginning of the loop giving the illusion of continuously advancing snake of videos. You can see overall structure of the video, concentrate on one point, or watch entire video by following one thumbnail.

8,275,745 Secure incremental updates to hierarchically structured information
In this scheme, a hierarchically structured file (e.g. XML or JSON) is represented as a flat set of triples of the form “elementId parentId elementValue”. Each triple is then encrypted into a pair like so “elementId encrypt(nonce, parentId,elementValue)”. The envisaged system is where an untrusted system is storing a large hierarchical tree, and a a trusted system wants to make incremental updates to it.
8,208,477 Data-dependent overlay network
This is an extension of standard peer-to-peer overlay P2P networks that can be more resilient to network partition. In a standard distributed hash table system, data objects are placed on different devices (“peers”) around the network without regard to how those data objects will be used.
